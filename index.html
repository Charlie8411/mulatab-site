FAQ

Questions, answered.

Is Mulatab a bank? +
No. Mulatab partners with a regulated banking-as-a-service provider (like the same kind powering Cash App and Chime) to handle money movement and FDIC-insured holding. We focus on the product experience; they handle the money plumbing.
How is this different from Venmo? +
Venmo is built for one-off payments between two people, with a public feed nobody asked for. Mulatab is built for groups — every Tab tracks the whole event (trip, dinner, household), automatically minimizes how many transfers it takes to settle everyone, and never publishes your transactions to strangers.
How does Mulatab make money? +
Three honest ways: an optional $4.99/mo Mulatab+ subscription for power-user features, an optional 1.5% Instant Mulatab fee if you want money to arrive in seconds instead of 1-2 days, and an upcoming Mulatab Card debit card. We never sell data or run ads inside the app.
When does Mulatab launch? +
Private beta is live now (invite-only). Public iOS launch is targeted for June 2026. Android follows in late 2026. Waitlist members get first access in launch order.
Is my data and money safe? +
Yes. We use Plaid for bank linking — the same encryption layer used by Venmo, Robinhood, and Chime. Your bank credentials never touch our servers. Held funds are FDIC-insured through our banking partner up to $250k. Two-factor authentication is required for all transfers over $100.
Do my friends have to use Mulatab too? +
Not initially. You can track a tab with anyone, and friends without Mulatab get a deep link to view the tab. To actually receive money, they'll need to sign up — which takes about 90 seconds. Most groups end up fully onboarded after the first trip.
